Elevation

Elevation communicates hierarchy — what's in front of what. OCL uses the MUI v5 default elevation scale, which is based on Material Design's 25-level shadow system. In practice, TBv3 today only uses level 1 (via Paper elevation={1} and the :hover lift on cards).

Per the April 2026 review, the Elevation Zeplin design lists all levels but "the first one is used, that's it." Use level 1 for raised panels, hover states, and interactive cards. Reach for higher levels only when a floating UI element needs to visually break out of the page flow (e.g. dialogs, popovers, tooltips).
